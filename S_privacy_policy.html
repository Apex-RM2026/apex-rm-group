Section 01

Introduction

Apex R&M Group Ltd ("we," "us," "our," or "the Company") is committed to protecting your privacy and handling your personal data responsibly, transparently, and in accordance with applicable data protection laws.

This Privacy Policy explains what information we collect, why we collect it, how we use and protect it, and what rights you have regarding your data. It applies to all users, visitors, clients, partners, and followers who interact with any of our digital services, websites, social media channels, or automated systems.

By accessing our website at apexrmgroup.com, using our social media automation tools, engaging with our social media profiles, or communicating with us through any channel, you acknowledge that you have read and understood this Privacy Policy and agree to its terms.

Quick Summary

We collect only what we need, use it only for legitimate purposes, never sell your data, and always give you control. This policy covers our website, all social media platforms, the Automation system, and any related tools we operate.

Section 04

Information We Collect

We collect information in the following categories, always using the minimum necessary for our stated purposes:

4.1 Information You Provide Directly

  • Identity Data: Full name, job title, organization name, professional credentials
  • Contact Data: Email address, phone number, WhatsApp number, physical address
  • Communication Data: Messages, inquiries, feedback, and comments submitted through our contact forms, email, or social media
  • Project & Consultation Data: Spatial data, project briefs, geographic coordinates, land parcel information, or environmental data you share during service delivery
  • Career Data: CVs, cover letters, education history, and employment records submitted through our careers page
  • Payment Data: Billing information required to process service payments (processed through secure third-party processors; we do not store raw card data)
  • Newsletter Data: Email address and preferences for subscription to our updates

4.2 Information We Collect Automatically

  • Technical Data: IP address, browser type and version, operating system, device identifiers, time zone, screen resolution
  • Usage Data: Pages visited, time spent, click paths, referral sources, search terms used within our site
  • Performance Data: Page load times, error logs, server response times
  • Cookie Data: Session identifiers, preference cookies, analytics cookies (see Section 10)

4.3 Information from Third Parties & APIs

  • LinkedIn API Data: Organization page analytics, follower demographics, post engagement metrics, comment data from our Company Page, and data accessible via the LinkedIn Community Management API (see Section 6)
  • Social Media Analytics: Aggregated, anonymized engagement data from Facebook Insights, X Analytics, Instagram Insights, and YouTube Analytics
  • Partner Referrals: Contact information shared with us by partners or clients with your knowledge and consent
  • Public Professional Profiles: Publicly available information from professional directories or social networks relevant to business inquiries
Data Minimization Principle

We are committed to collecting only the minimum personal data necessary for the specific, legitimate purpose for which it is being processed. We do not collect sensitive personal data (health, race, political opinions, etc.) unless explicitly required and consented to.

LinkedIn Community Management API

Apex R&M Group Ltd uses the LinkedIn Community Management API to manage our LinkedIn Company Page, engage with our professional community, and publish content in compliance with LinkedIn's Developer Agreement and Privacy Policy.

6.1 API Permissions & Scope

Our LinkedIn API integration requests the following permissions, used strictly for the stated purposes:

Permission / Scope Purpose Data Accessed
r_organization_social Read Company Page posts and analytics Post performance metrics, engagement rates, impressions
w_organization_social Publish posts to Company Page Content we publish; no personal member data

r_1st_connections_size Understand audience size Aggregate follower count only
w_member_social Manage community interactions on behalf of authorized admins Comments, reactions made by our page admins

6.2 Data Accessed via LinkedIn API

Through the LinkedIn Community Management API, we access and process:

  • Company Page Analytics: Impressions, clicks, engagement rates, follower demographics (aggregated by LinkedIn, not individually identifiable)
  • Post Performance Data: Likes, comments, shares, and reach for content we publish
  • Comment Content: Text of comments on our Company Page posts, used to respond to community members and monitor engagement
  • Follower Growth Data: Changes in follower count over time (aggregate only)
  • Visitor Analytics: Aggregated data on page visitors by industry, function, seniority, and geography — provided by LinkedIn in anonymized form

6.3 Limitations & Commitments

  • We do not use the LinkedIn API to scrape or store personal data of individual LinkedIn members without their explicit consent
  • We do not use LinkedIn API data for purposes beyond managing our Company Page and community engagement
  • We do not sell, rent, or transfer LinkedIn-sourced data to any third party
  • All LinkedIn data is processed in accordance with LinkedIn's API Terms of Service and Data Processing Agreement
  • Our API access tokens are stored securely and rotated regularly
  • We respond to LinkedIn API data deletion requests within 30 days

6.4 OAuth & User Authorization

If you authorize our application to access your LinkedIn account (e.g., as a Company Page administrator), we request only the minimum necessary permissions. You may revoke access at any time through LinkedIn's Security Settings at linkedin.com/psettings/permitted-services.

LinkedIn Developer Compliance

Apex R&M Group Ltd is committed to full compliance with LinkedIn's Developer Agreement, API Terms of Use, and Community Guidelines. Our use of the LinkedIn API is subject to LinkedIn's Privacy Policy available at linkedin.com/legal/privacy-policy.

Section 08

Legal Basis for Processing

We process personal data only where we have a valid legal basis. The applicable bases are:

  • Consent (Art. 6(1)(a) GDPR): Where you have given freely, specific, informed, and unambiguous consent — for example, newsletter subscription or cookie use. You may withdraw consent at any time.
  • Contract Performance (Art. 6(1)(b) GDPR): Where processing is necessary to fulfill a contract with you or to take pre-contractual steps at your request — for example, delivering consulting services.
  • Legal Obligation (Art. 6(1)(c) GDPR): Where we are required to process data to comply with a legal obligation — for example, tax records, audit requirements, or government requests.
  • Legitimate Interests (Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR): Where processing is necessary for our legitimate business interests, provided these are not overridden by your rights — for example, website analytics, social media management, fraud prevention, and API integrations for our own operations.
  • Rwandan Data Protection Law: We comply with Rwanda's Law on the Protection of Personal Data and Privacy (Law No. 058/2021 of 13/10/2021) and regulations issued thereunder.

Section 12

Data Security

We implement robust technical and organizational security measures to protect your data against unauthorized access, loss, destruction, or alteration:

Technical Measures

  • Encryption in Transit: All data transmitted between your browser and our servers is encrypted via TLS 1.3 / HTTPS
  • Encryption at Rest: Sensitive data stored in our Supabase database is encrypted at rest using AES-256
  • Access Controls: Role-based access control (RBAC) ensures only authorized personnel access specific data
  • API Key Security: LinkedIn and other API keys/tokens are stored in environment variables, never in source code, and rotated regularly
  • Authentication: Our Automation System requires secure login with session management and timeout policies
  • Cloudflare Protection: DDoS mitigation, WAF (Web Application Firewall), and bot management via Cloudflare

Organizational Measures

  • Data processing agreements (DPAs) with all third-party processors
  • Staff training on data protection and privacy practices
  • Internal access limited on a need-to-know basis
  • Regular security reviews of our systems and third-party processors
  • Incident response procedure for data breaches
Data Breach Notification

In the event of a personal data breach that is likely to result in a high risk to your rights and freedoms, we will notify affected individuals without undue delay, and notify the relevant supervisory authority within 72 hours, as required by applicable law.

Section 13

Your Rights

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have the following rights regarding your personal data. We are committed to honoring these rights promptly and without charge:

Right of Access

Request a copy of the personal data we hold about you and information about how we use it.

Right to Rectification

Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete personal data we hold about you.

Right to Erasure

Request deletion of your personal data where there is no compelling reason for its continued processing.

Right to Restrict

Request that we limit how we use your data in certain circumstances while we address a complaint.

Right to Portability

Receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format and transfer it to another controller.

Right to Object

Object to processing based on legitimate interests, including profiling and direct marketing.

Withdraw Consent

Withdraw consent at any time where processing is based on consent, without affecting prior lawful processing.

Right to Complain

Lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority if you believe we have not handled your data lawfully.

How to Exercise Your Rights

To exercise any of these rights, please contact our Data Protection Officer using the details in Section 18. Please include your full name, contact details, and a clear description of your request. We will respond within 30 days and may need to verify your identity before processing the request.

If you are not satisfied with our response, you have the right to lodge a complaint with Rwanda's National Cyber Security Authority (NCSA) or the relevant data protection authority in your jurisdiction.

Section 14

International Data Transfers

Apex R&M Group Ltd is based in Rwanda. Some of our third-party service providers (including Cloudflare, Supabase, LinkedIn, and Google) may process data in the United States, the European Union, or other jurisdictions. Where we transfer data internationally, we ensure appropriate safeguards are in place:

  • Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs): We use EU-approved SCCs where applicable with processors in countries without an adequacy decision
  • Adequacy Decisions: We transfer to countries recognized as providing adequate protection where available
  • Processor DPAs: All processors sign Data Processing Agreements incorporating appropriate transfer mechanisms
  • LinkedIn: Governed by LinkedIn's Data Processing Agreement and their participation in applicable data transfer frameworks

You may request details of the specific safeguards we rely on for any international transfer by contacting us at privacy@apexrmgroup.com.
